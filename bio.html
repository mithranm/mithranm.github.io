Introductory Bio

Mithran Mohanraj

My name is Mithran Mohanraj and I am from Aldie, VA. I am a senior at George Mason and my major is Computer Science.

The path to the Computer Science major was a relatively straightforward one. It helped that my father was a software developer who made six figures sitting at a computer all day. I can sit at the computer all day too, I thought. I thought majoring in computer science with the intent to be a professional software developer was the only rational decision. I thought my professional journey would be straightforward and easy. But I thought wrong.

The abrupt shift to online learning triggered by COVID-19 was detrimental to me, and my situation worsened when in-person classes resumed in the fall of 2021. As a last-ditch effort, I transferred to George Mason.

Coincidentally, this transfer occurred shortly after a service called “ChatGPT” was released by OpenAI. I had used chatbots such as Mitsuku and Cleverbot before, but ChatGPT was in a league of its own. I was far from a power user, however, I just treated it like an alternative to Stackoverflow.com. That was until my Summer 2024 internship at Peraton Inc. where I was tasked with “investigating how AI tools can assist in the conversion of COBOL to Java”. This internship sparked an obsession with generative AI, and I am now enrolled in CS 399 - Issues in Gov and Politics to explore how AI will reshape wealth distribution and economic structures.
